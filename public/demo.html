Solar Panel Efficiency Explained

Modern monocrystalline panels achieve 20-22% efficiency, converting roughly one-fifth of incident sunlight into electricity. Polycrystalline panels sit around 15-17%, while thin-film technologies range from 10-13%.

Why Does Panel Wattage Matter?

Higher wattage panels produce more power per square metre, reducing the number of panels needed for a given system size.

Key Benefits of Solar PV

  • Lower electricity bills — Generate your own power and reduce grid dependence by up to 70%.
  • Environmental impact — A typical 6.6 kW system offsets roughly 7 tonnes of CO₂ per year.
  • Property value — Homes with solar sell for 4-6% more on average (Zillow, 2023).
  • Government incentives — STCs (Small-scale Technology Certificates) can reduce upfront cost by $3,000-$5,000.
  • Energy independence — Pair with a battery to store excess generation for night-time use.

Understanding Your Quote

Good to know
Most residential quotes in Australia range from $4,500 to $9,000 for a 6.6 kW system after STC rebates. Always compare at least three quotes before committing.
Watch out
Avoid quotes that seem significantly cheaper than the market average. Low-cost systems may use Tier 2 panels or inverters with shorter warranties, costing more in the long run.

How Solar Installation Works

  1. Site assessment A certified installer visits your property to evaluate roof orientation, shading, and electrical switchboard capacity.
  2. System design The installer designs a system tailored to your energy usage, roof space, and budget. You receive a detailed quote.
  3. Approvals & paperwork Your installer handles grid connection applications and STC rebate paperwork with the Clean Energy Regulator.
  4. Installation day A team of CEC-accredited electricians mounts the panels, installs the inverter, and connects everything to your switchboard. Typically completed in 4-8 hours.
  5. Inspection & activation After a final electrical inspection, your distributor activates the system and you begin generating your own electricity.
