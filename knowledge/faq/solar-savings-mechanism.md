# How Will Solar Reduce My Electricity Bill?

Solar savings come first from self-consumption: during daylight hours, your property uses electricity generated on-site instead of buying the same units from TNB. Excess generation can be exported under Solar ATAP and credited at the applicable export rate, subject to the Monthly Allowed Quantity and programme rules. Export credits reset monthly, so correct system sizing and daytime energy use matter. Solar can reduce energy charges substantially, but it does not automatically eliminate fixed charges, levies, taxes, maximum-demand charges, or every ringgit on the bill.

## Supporting Facts

- Solar ATAP allows for exported energy to be credited against the bill up to a Monthly Allowed Quantity (MAQ) (Source: `knowledge/schemes/solar-atap-malaysia-complete-guide.md`)
- Net Billing Formula calculates savings based on self-consumption and export credits (Source: `knowledge/schemes/solar-atap-malaysia-complete-guide.md`)
- Solar credits CANNOT offset Maximum Demand, fixed charges, KWTBB levy, or Service Tax (SST) (Source: `knowledge/schemes/solar-atap-malaysia-complete-guide.md`)
