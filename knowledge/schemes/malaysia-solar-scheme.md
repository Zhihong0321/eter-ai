# Malaysia Solar Scheme: Net Energy Metering (NEM)

## What Is NEM?

Net Energy Metering (NEM) is Malaysia's flagship solar incentive scheme administered by the Energy Commission (Suruhanjaya Tenaga). Under NEM, solar PV system owners can export excess electricity generated by their panels back to the national grid. The exported energy is credited against their electricity bill, effectively using the grid as a virtual battery. This mechanism allows homeowners to significantly reduce or even eliminate their monthly electricity costs.

## How It Works

When your solar panels produce more electricity than your home consumes, the surplus flows into the TNB grid and your meter runs backward, accumulating credits. At night or during cloudy periods, you draw electricity from the grid as normal. At the end of each billing cycle, only the net consumption is billed. Under the current NEM 3.0 program, credits are calculated on a one-to-one basis at the prevailing tariff rate, making solar economics highly attractive.

## Eligibility

NEM is available to all TNB account holders across Peninsular Malaysia, including residential, commercial, and industrial users. For residential installations, the typical system size ranges from 4kWp to 10kWp, depending on roof area and electricity consumption patterns. Properties must have a valid TNB account with no outstanding arrears.

## Application Process

1. Engage a certified solar installer registered with SEDA (Sustainable Energy Development Authority)
2. The installer conducts a site survey and designs the system
3. Submit NEM application through the MyNEM portal
4. Upon approval, installation proceeds (typically 2-4 weeks)
5. TNB installs a bidirectional meter
6. System commissioning and handover

## SelCo for Selangor

Residents in Selangor benefit from the Selangor Solar Programme (SelCo), which offers additional incentives and streamlined approval processes. SelCo provides rebates and financing options specifically for Selangor residents, making solar adoption even more accessible.

## Bill Impact

A standard residential system can reduce imported electricity during daylight hours and may generate export credits under the applicable scheme. The actual bill impact depends on system size, daytime usage, export rules, and site conditions, so the final estimate should be confirmed by a sales consultant.
