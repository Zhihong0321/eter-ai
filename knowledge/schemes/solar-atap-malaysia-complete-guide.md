# Solar ATAP Malaysia: Complete Guide to Malaysia's New Rooftop Solar Scheme

## What is Solar ATAP?

The **Solar Accelerated Transition Action Programme (Solar ATAP)** is Malaysia's new rooftop solar framework that officially launched on January 1, 2026, replacing the Net Energy Metering (NEM 3.0) scheme. Administered by PETRA, SEDA Malaysia, and the Energy Commission, Solar ATAP introduces a cost-neutral, open-access model designed to maintain grid equity while scaling distributed solar PV capacity in Peninsular Malaysia.

### Key Changes from NEM 3.0

| Feature | NEM 3.0 | Solar ATAP |
|---------|---------|------------|
| **Quota Limit** | Capped (e.g., 600 MW residential) | No fixed national capacity ceiling |
| **Export Rate** | 1-to-1 retail tariff offset | Energy Charge rate (~RM 0.218 to RM 0.37/kWh) |
| **Credit Rollover** | Up to 12 months | Monthly reset (unused credits forfeited) |
| **Commercial Export** | Fixed avoided-cost billing | Average System Marginal Price (SMP) |
| **Commercial Capacity** | 75% to 85% of Maximum Demand | Up to 100% of Maximum Demand (max 1 MWac) |

## Eligibility Criteria

Solar ATAP is open to all registered TNB account holders in Peninsular Malaysia:

- **Domestic (Residential):** Single-phase up to 5 kWac; three-phase up to 15 kWac
- **Commercial/Industrial:** Up to 100% of Maximum Demand, capped at 1 MWac
- **Properties:** Single-tenant only with clear roof rights
- **Excluded:** Multi-tenant buildings, sub-meter setups, and high-rises (except single-tenant properties with large roof spaces for common facilities)

## Billing and Net Billing Calculation

Under Solar ATAP, self-consumption is the primary economic driver. Exported energy is credited at lower rates than retail import rates.

### Net Billing Formula

```
Net Energy Charge = (Imported Energy × Retail Tariff) − (Exported Energy × Export Credit Rate)
```

### Export Credit Rates

**Domestic (Residential Tariff A):**
- Low consumption (<1,500 kWh/month): ~RM 0.218 to RM 0.27 per kWh
- High consumption (≥1,500 kWh/month): ~RM 0.37 per kWh

**Commercial/Industrial:**
- Based on Average System Marginal Price (SMP), typically RM 0.20 to RM 0.40 per kWh

### Monthly Allowed Quantity (MAQ)

Credits are capped by the MAQ formula:
```
MAQ = Installed Capacity (kWac) × Peak Sun-Hours × Billing Days
```

Any exported energy exceeding MAQ receives **zero credit**.

### What Solar Credits CANNOT Offset

- Maximum Demand or capacity charges
- Fixed monthly customer or network service charges
- Surcharges or penalties
- 1.6% Renewable Energy (KWTBB) fund levy
- 8% Service Tax (SST) for usage exceeding 600 kWh
- Monthly Automatic Fuel Adjustment (AFA) rate

## Financial Incentives

### Residential Incentives

**1. 70% Capacity Subsidy (up to RM 9,800)**
- Payback period: approximately 2.5 to 3 years

**2. SuRIA Home Programme**
- Direct cash rebate: **RM 600 per kWac**, capped at RM 3,000
- Available for systems ≥5 kWac
- Funding pool: RM 150 million (250 MW national quota)
- Open to Malaysian citizens on Domestic Tariff A
- **Deadline: December 31, 2026**
- Registration starts June 1, 2026
- Cannot be combined with SolaRIS rebate

### Commercial & Industrial Incentives

**Green Investment Tax Allowance (GITA):**
- 60% allowance on qualifying solar capital expenditure
- Offset up to 70% of statutory income per Year of Assessment
- Valid until December 31, 2026
- Requirements: Outright purchase or hire-purchase loan, MyHIJAU-certified equipment, GTV certificate from MGTC

**Green Technology Financing Scheme 5.0 (GTFS 5.0):**
- Government-backed guarantee of up to 80% on green commercial loans
- Enables lower interest rates for businesses

## Application Process

Applications must be submitted through SEDA's **eATAP portal** via a SEDA-registered PV Service Provider (RPVSP).

### Step-by-Step Process

1. **Load Profile & Bill Analysis** – RPVSP analyzes historical billing data to size the system
2. **Technical Site Assessment** – Roof orientation, structural integrity, shading, main distribution board rating
3. **Technical Studies** (if required) – CCC, CAS, or PSS coordination with TNB
4. **Online Submission via eATAP** – Submit applicant's profile, MyKad/SSM documents, Single Line Diagram, latest TNB bill, and pay application fee (RM 7.50 per kW installed)
5. **Smart Metering & Commissioning** – TNB installs bi-directional smart meter; 10-year contract begins on commissioning date

### Technical Assessment Fees

| Study | Required For | Fee (RM) |
|-------|--------------|----------|
| **CCC (Connection Confirmation Check)** | Domestic >5 kWac (single-phase) or >15 kWac (three-phase) | 1,000 |
| **CAS (Connection Assessment Study)** | Commercial >72 kWac | 1,000–8,000 (based on capacity) |
| **PSS (Power System Study)** | HV connections >425 kWac | 15,000 |

> **Note:** If studies reveal grid cannot accommodate the system, applicant must cover all grid upgrade and transformer reinforcement costs.

### Important Deadlines

- Installation must be completed within **18 months** of SEDA approval, or the permit is revoked

## Residential System Sizing & Costs

System size is typically matched to daytime load, roof area, and approved export limits. A site assessment is the correct way to determine whether a home needs a smaller, standard, or larger configuration.

## Avoiding Solar Scams

Since Solar ATAP launch, reported solar scams in Malaysia have increased by **300%**. Protect yourself:

- **Verify RPVSP Registration:** Check SEDA's official 2026 Registered PV Service Provider directory
- **Verify Equipment Certification:** Confirm panels and inverters are listed in MGTC's MyHIJAU green directory
- **Use Trusted Installers:** Partner with established providers to ensure safety and system reliability

## Key Recommendations

**For Residential Homeowners:**
- Size systems for daytime load, not maximum roof coverage
- Shift high-energy appliances to peak sun hours (11 AM–3 PM)
- Choose reducing balance loans over flat-rate financing
- Commission before December 31, 2026 to qualify for SuRIA Home rebate

**For Commercial & Industrial:**
- Combine GITA and GTFS 5.0 for maximum financial benefit
- Conduct grid capacity studies early in the design phase
- Consider Battery Energy Storage Systems (BESS) for facilities with high peak evening demand
