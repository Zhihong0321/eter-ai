# Solar PV Basics

## How Solar Panels Generate Electricity

Solar photovoltaic (PV) technology converts sunlight directly into electricity using semiconductor materials, primarily silicon. When photons from sunlight strike the solar cell, they knock electrons loose from their atoms, creating an electrical current. This phenomenon is known as the photovoltaic effect, discovered in 1839 by French physicist Edmond Becquerel.

## System Components

A complete solar PV system consists of several key components working together:

- **Solar Panels**: Arrays of solar cells mounted on rooftops or ground structures that capture sunlight and generate direct current (DC) electricity.
- **Inverter**: Converts DC electricity from the panels into alternating current (AC) used by household appliances and the national grid.
- **Energy Meter**: Measures the flow of electricity between your home and the grid, enabling net metering credits.
- **Grid Connection**: Links your system to the national power grid (TNB in Malaysia), allowing you to export surplus electricity and import when needed.

## How Electricity Is Generated

The process begins when sunlight hits the silicon cells in solar panels. Each cell contains two layers of silicon — one with a positive charge (P-type) and one with a negative charge (N-type). The junction between these layers creates an electric field that forces freed electrons to flow in one direction, producing DC electricity. The inverter then converts this into AC power at 240V/50Hz, matching Malaysia's grid standard.

## Malaysia's Equatorial Advantage

Malaysia's position near the equator provides exceptional solar potential. The country receives an average of 4.5 to 5.5 peak sun hours per day throughout the year, with relatively consistent solar irradiance across seasons. This means solar panels in Malaysia can generate electricity reliably year-round, making solar PV one of the most viable renewable energy sources in the region. The tropical climate, while occasionally cloudy, still allows for significant energy production even on overcast days due to diffuse radiation.
